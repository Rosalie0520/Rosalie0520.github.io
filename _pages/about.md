I am a first-year PhD student in Film and Media Studies at the University of California, Berkeley. I held a B.A. from Northwestern University with high honors in Comparative Literature and Radio/TV/Film and a M.A. in Asian Studies at Berkeley.  

My research revolves around the concept of transformation, which includes medium revolution and inventions, as well as the constant phase-shifts of forms engineered by technological and biotechnological attempts. I am specifically interested in exploring how phase-shifts at different scales - from cellular agriculture to (inter)national media infrastructure rebuild - affect the environment while becoming parts of the milieu for the next transformations. I grapple with how the medium-milieu coupling creates episteme and power while producing wastes and failures.
